Segment Guide

Every response consists of 4 segments (S1-S4) that complete the Phase 1 subject. Each segment serves a specific purpose for voice delivery.

Segment Structure

S1 - Headline/Predicate: Concise predicate (40-80 chars) that directly completes the subject. Voice-optimized, lowercase, verb-start.
S2 - Clinical Details: Additional clinical context, monitoring requirements, or key considerations.
S3 - Context/Safety: Mechanism, safety warnings, or important contextual information.
S4 - Source: Source attribution (e.g., "Source: FDA DailyMed").

Examples by Intent

Interaction
Subject: "The interaction between warfarin and amiodarone..."
S1: "significantly increases INR and bleeding risk"
S2: "Monitor INR closely when starting or stopping amiodarone; warfarin dose reduction often needed"
S3: "Amiodarone inhibits CYP2C9 metabolism of warfarin, leading to elevated levels"
S4: "Source: FDA DailyMed"
Dosing
Subject: "The dose of ketamine for RSI..."
S1: "is 1 to 2 milligrams per kilogram IV push"
S2: "Onset within 30 to 60 seconds; duration 10 to 20 minutes"
S3: "Use lower end of range in hypotension or shock; may cause transient hypertension"
S4: "Source: AHA ACLS Guidelines; DailyMed"
Contraindication
Subject: "Contraindications for lisinopril..."
S1: "include pregnancy, angioedema history, and concomitant aliskiren use in diabetes"
S2: "Discontinue immediately if pregnancy detected; do not use with sacubitril within 36 hours"
S3: "ACE inhibitors can cause fetal renal dysfunction and death; angioedema risk increased with sacubitril"
S4: "Source: FDA DailyMed"
Pregnancy
Subject: "Use of warfarin in pregnancy..."
S1: "is contraindicated except in women with mechanical heart valves at high risk of thromboembolism"
S2: "Can cause fetal warfarin syndrome in first trimester and bleeding in third trimester"
S3: "Consider heparin alternatives; if warfarin required, use lowest effective dose with close monitoring"
S4: "Source: FDA DailyMed"
Renal Dosing
Subject: "Renal dosing for vancomycin..."
S1: "requires dose reduction and interval extension based on creatinine clearance"
S2: "For CrCl 20-49: reduce dose by 50% or extend interval to 24-48 hours"
S3: "Monitor trough levels closely; nephrotoxicity risk increases with impaired renal function"
S4: "Source: FDA DailyMed"

Key Principles

  • S1 must be a predicate that grammatically completes the subject
  • S1 should be concise (40-80 chars ideal) and voice-optimized
  • All segments use lowercase except proper nouns and acronyms
  • Voice delivery: Subject + S1 + S2 + S3 + S4 flows naturally when spoken
  • Clinical accuracy is paramount - never sacrifice correctness for brevity

Scoring Criteria

5 - Excellent

Perfect clinical accuracy, ideal phrasing for voice delivery, complete information. Ready for production with no changes needed.

4 - Good

Accurate and clear, but minor phrasing tweaks could improve voice delivery or conciseness.

3 - Acceptable

Clinically correct but could be clearer or more concise. May be awkward when spoken aloud.

2 - Needs Improvement

Technically correct but awkward phrasing, missing key context, or too verbose for voice delivery.

1 - Poor

Technically correct but severely lacking in clarity, usability, or appropriate tone.

0 - Incomprehensible

Clinically inaccurate, confusing, or completely unusable. Flag for immediate review.
